# The One Tool I Use to Save TikToks and Instagram Reels (No App, No Watermark)

Let me tell you about a problem that sounds small but becomes genuinely annoying at scale.

I use TikTok and Instagram a lot — not just scrolling, but actually referencing content. Tutorials I want to rewatch offline. Workout clips I save as inspiration. Recipe videos I want on my phone when I'm standing in a kitchen with flour on my hands and no internet connection.

For years I relied on TikTok's native save button and Instagram's bookmark system. Both work fine *inside the apps*. The moment you want an actual file — a real video sitting in your Camera Roll — both options fall apart completely.

TikTok's saved videos come stamped with a massive watermark. Instagram's saves are bookmarks, not files. And every time I found a third-party app that worked, it either plastered its own watermark over the video, started asking me to watch ads, or broke when the platforms updated their APIs.

---

## What I Use Now

I landed on [DropZap](https://www.dropzap.digital) about six months ago and haven't switched since. It's a website — no app install — and it handles both TikTok and Instagram from the same interface.

The workflow is stupid simple:

**For TikTok:**
1. Open TikTok, tap Share on the video, tap Copy Link
2. Open DropZap in Safari or Chrome, tap the TikTok tab
3. Paste the link, tap Download
4. The MP4 saves to my Downloads folder — no watermark, no logo

**For Instagram Reels:**
1. Open Instagram, tap the three-dot menu on the Reel, tap Copy Link
2. DropZap defaults to the Instagram tab — paste the link, tap Download
3. Same result: clean MP4 in my Downloads

**For Instagram carousels (multi-photo posts):**
DropZap downloads all the slides as a ZIP file. I unzip it and get every image individually. This is genuinely useful for reference posts where the creator put useful info across 8 slides.

## The One Caveat

It only works on public accounts. If a creator's account is private, the video isn't accessible via share link, and no downloader can reach it. This applies to every tool — it's not a DropZap limitation.

Also, TikTok periodically changes its internal APIs, which means any TikTok downloader can go down for a few hours after a major TikTok update. DropZap has been the most consistently up of the tools I've tried, but no tool is 100% uptime. If it's ever down, ssstik or SnapTik are reasonable backups.
