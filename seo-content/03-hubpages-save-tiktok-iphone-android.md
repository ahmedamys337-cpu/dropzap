# How to Save TikTok Videos on iPhone and Android Without Watermark (Step-by-Step)

Saving a TikTok video to your phone sounds like it should be simple — and it is, once you know which method actually works. TikTok's built-in save option leaves a watermark. Here's how to get a clean, watermark-free copy on both iPhone and Android.

---

## On iPhone (iOS)

### Method A: Using Safari (Recommended)

1. **Copy the TikTok link.** Open TikTok, find the video, tap the **Share** icon (arrow pointing right), then tap **Copy link**.

2. **Open Safari.** (This must be Safari — iOS file downloads work best in Safari.)

3. **Go to the downloader.** In the address bar, type `dropzap.digital` and open it.

4. **Select TikTok tab.** The site defaults to Instagram — tap the **TikTok** tab in the navigation.

5. **Paste and download.** Paste your link into the box, tap **Download**.

6. **Save to Files.** The MP4 will prompt you to open in Files app. Once in Files, you can move it to **iCloud Drive** or share it directly.

7. **Move to Camera Roll (optional).** Open the Files app, find the downloaded video, tap and hold it, choose **Share** → **Save Video**. It will appear in your Photos app.

**Total time:** Under 30 seconds.

Works the same as above in Chrome, Firefox, or Brave on iOS. The file lands in your Downloads folder within the browser, then you can move it to Files.

## On Android

Android is more straightforward than iPhone for downloads.

## Common Problems and Fixes

### "The video won't save to my Camera Roll on iPhone"

This is a common iPhone quirk. The video saves to the Files app first. To move it to Camera Roll:
1. Open **Files** app
2. Navigate to **Downloads** (or **On My iPhone** → **Downloads**)
3. Long-press the video file → **Share** → **Save Video**

### "The link says 'Invalid URL'"

TikTok share links look like `https://vm.tiktok.com/XXXXXX/` or `https://www.tiktok.com/@username/video/123`. Both formats work. Make sure you're copying the link from the Share menu — not just copying the URL from the address bar inside TikTok's browser.

### "The download tool shows an error"

TikTok updates its internal API frequently. If one tool fails, try another. DropZap, ssstik, and SnapTik are the three most maintained tools — if one is down, at least one of the others usually works.

### "The video is lower quality than what I see in the app"

TikTok controls the quality served via its API. Some videos are only available at lower quality outside the app. This is TikTok's decision, not something the downloader controls.
